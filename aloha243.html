Here is what happened on aloha airlines flight 243.

The crew flew on 6 flights and reported that they were all uneventful.

At 1325 aloha airlines flight 243 took off from hilo airport.

When the aircraft leveled at 24,000 feet, the pilots heard a loud clap and a moment later a they heard wind.

The captain noticed that the cockpit door was missing and that the first class ceiling was gone.

The captain started an emergency descent. the No. 1 flight attendant was swept out of the aircraft.

The first officer tuned the transponder to 7700. She tried to let honolulu ARTCC know that they were diverting to maui but it was so loud she couldn't here any transmissions.

Even though the ARTCC did not get the initial transmission, the controller did notice a transponder with the code 7700 so the controller tried to contact the flight several times without success.
